Meet Jessica Jeson

Jessica Jeson is the Sound Librarian at Sound Library. She works closely with residents to gather and document local stories, environmental sounds, and heritage through audio recordings.

Why Her Role Matters

Her work turns the library into more than just a building—it becomes a shared space of memory and culture. Through universal recording sessions and outreach, Jessica Jeson helps ensure that every voice has a place in digital archive.

Her Impact

  • Organizes soundwalks and oral history workshops
  • Supports marginal voices to be heard
  • Builds trust between the library and universal storytellers
  • Trains volunteers in basic sound documentation

She brings sound to life—connecting past and present through listening, sharing, and preservation.

“Every voice matters — I'm here to help preserve them.”
— Jessica Jeson, Sound Librarian
